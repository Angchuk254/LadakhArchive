Overview

A Living Laboratory at the Roof of the World

Ladakh's landscapes range from scorching cold deserts at 3,000m to permanent glaciers above 7,000m — creating an extraordinary mosaic of ecosystems where uniquely adapted species have evolved to survive conditions that would be fatal to most life on Earth.

The Trans-Himalayan region is home to over 73 mammal species, 320+ bird species, and 800+ plant species. From the world's highest-density snow leopard habitat to the only Indian breeding grounds of the black-necked crane, Ladakh is a biodiversity treasure chest of global significance.
