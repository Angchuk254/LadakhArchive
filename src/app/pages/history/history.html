Overview

A History Spanning Millennia

Ladakh — meaning "Land of High Passes" — sits at the intersection of the Silk Road, the Tibetan Plateau, and the Indian subcontinent. For millennia, it has been a melting pot where Buddhist monks, Central Asian traders, Tibetan warriors, Mughal emperors, and Dogra generals all left their mark.

Its history is not just one of conquests and treaties, but also of monasteries built on cliffs, trade caravans crossing the Karakoram, and a unique cultural identity that has endured through every upheaval.
